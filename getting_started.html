The Basics

Lets start some basics knowledge about the cards in Magic: The Gathering. Firstly magic cards are represented by 5 colours: White, Blue, Black, Red, and Green. Each of these colours tend to follow a theme or style of gameplay.

The colours of your cards will also indicate the conditions needed to cast them.
Let's breakdown the parts of the card below:

What are the phases of a turn?

  1. Untap
  2. Upkeep
  3. Draw
  4. Main Phase 1
  5. Combat Phase
  6. Main Phase 2
  7. End Step

What happens during each phase?

At the beginning of the turn there are three phases before you start playing most lands and spells.
The Untap phase where you untap all the cards you control in play (A.K.A permanents).
The Upkeep phase where things will sometimes happen.
The Draw phase where you will take the top card of you deck and put it into your hand (this is called drawing a card).

Here they are summarized in the table below:

What happens here? Untap Upkeep Draw
Can I cast spells? No Only Instants and Cards with Flash Only Instants and Cards with Flash
Can I activate abilities No Yes (unless they are Sorcery speed only) Yes (unless they are Sorcery speed only)
What do I do? Untap all Permanents you control Nothing unless a card specifies Draw a card

Note: Don't worry about the Upkeep for now, we'll get to that later.
